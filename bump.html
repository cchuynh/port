OVERVIEW

Bump addresses the difficulty associated with missed in-person connections. While the global dating market has grown tremendously and is crowded, current offerings fail to capitalize on meaningful in-person connections that happen spontaneously in daily life.

Our solution, Bump, fills this gap by creating a platform for users to easily exchange contact information when they experience that "love-at-first-sight" moment with a stranger.

The team spent over five months creating and developing the business proposal behind Bump. This project can either be examined through a UX only lens or a UX + Business lens. The rationale was to showcase the intersectionality between the two fields on an emerging app.

OBJECTIVES



Market Analysis


The purpose is tTo understand the overall landscape and trends of the market, helping to identify opportunities and potential pitfalls for product design


Online dating platforms provide a digital space for people to find romantic connections through websites and apps, offering various services tailored to individual preferences.

Users create profiles, utilize matchmaking algorithms, and interact with potential partners, shaping an adaptable environment influenced by technology, society, and user activity. The market, valued at $8 million in 2022, is expected to see a 7.6% annual growth rate from 2023 to 2032.


Political/Legal

Dating companies must comply with consumer protection and data privacy laws, conduct background checks, and prioritize legal issues to prevent potential business derailment.

Economic

Despite being non-essential, dating apps are resilient during recessions, experiencing increased usage, and price wars exist due to competition, necessitating careful pricing strategies.

Social/Demographic

Cultural shifts, like increased acceptance of online dating post-COVID, impact user behavior, diversity movements lead to niche apps, and ethical concerns arise in connecting people.

Technology

AI enhances matchmaking algorithms, improves user experiences, and streamlines processes, making non-AI apps potentially obsolete and requiring minimal infrastructure changes.

Global

Dating apps transcend cultural boundaries, but cultural differences influence user behavior, requiring apps to navigate cultural nuances for inclusive experiences.

SURVEYS


Let's dive deeper into the user base



We brainstormed the potential target groups we wanted to tap into and focused our survey on gathering data on those assumptions.



Our goal was to target a specific user base to set as our first customer base. The reason behind that is because at first, the focus might be on attracting young adults due to their higher engagement with dating apps. They are often early adopters of technology (bump technology) and may provide valuable feedback to refine the app before targeting other age groups.






TARGET GROUP


We aimed to target the college student section first rather than the equally present opportunity in the newly employed section because we have the greatest access to expose this demographic initially. Additionally, this demographic will become the “Newly Employed” segment organically.



Through our research phase, we determined that college students were the best target group to select as our user base. The categories refer to the different criteria that we examined to determine which demographic was the best to fit.




This research was done looking at the market as a whole, and while that was beneficial, we figured… How about we learn more about our target groups in their most organic settings?
