Uber Tutors


Community-based solution to connect college campus students and expand on academic needs

OVERVIEW

College students need a fast and easy way to connect with a tutor when they are on a tight timeline.

If college students could be connected with a tutor within a short period of time, it would enhance studying experiences and be implemented throughout the higher education system.

Over the span of three weeks, I developed a deep passion for this case study which represents a revisionary approach to improve and gain my personal experience in UX Research and Design.








EMPATHIZE


Putting myself in the user's shoes



Three weeks ago, I sat in a Ross room, dreading my accounting exam. I had a million questions and no one to ask them to.


Then it occurred to me: Nearing exams, many students feel this way... What if there was an efficient way to find course-specific tutors on a tight timeline?













☺

PROBLEM STATEMENT

College students need an on-demand tutoring option to effectively prepare for their classes.

Wait a second, Cecilia - As a college student, aren't you a little biased?

In summary, my assumptions were that students want to succeed, might be unsure where to receive help, and are already using various tech platforms.

USER SURVEYS


Let's start talking.


I started by sending out a survey to students on my campus with prompts that would help me aide in the "what" of being a student and getting tutoring services.


I ended up receiving 24 responses from the Univeristy of Michigan students which I was able to synthesize.


FINDINGS

From 24 user feedbacks:

96%

struggled with at least one class

83%

cram at the last minute for classes due to busy schedules

79%

would appreciate tutoring by students who have succeeded

REVISED PROBLEM STATEMENT

College students need an on-demand tutoring option from students that have previously taken the class in order to effectively prepare for their classes in a quick and responsive timeline.

Who makes up the most of our users?

How many users are we impacting?

How are users accessing the product?




Using the application on mobile smartphones.





CORE DISCOVERIES


Pinpointing the issues.



Tech Familiarity

Use an established, well-known, and pre-existing platform offers similar services: Uber which connects you to car rides and food deliveries

Reliable connections

Provide monetary gain for tutors and educational help for tutees, calling on visualized elements of experience, rewards, and location

Solid user interaction

Get connected to students who have taken your classes in real time to receive reliable, fast, and in-person help

DESIGN DECISIONS


01: Uber Tutors Tab


Create an Uber Tutors tab into the original Uber app → allows users to get access to Uber Tutors without having to take more actions beforehand




02: Edit locations


Utilizes a majority of Uber's functionality already, stays consistent with Uber and Uber Eats, and allows users to adjust how far they are willing to travel if they want to meet their tutor in the middle




03: Filter searches


Short, sweet, and simple, this feature encourages users to make a more informed decision when it comes to finding a tutor that fits their needs using Uber algorithms to generate suggestions options but also shows a rating system for tutors.

UBER TUTORS



The connective system that allows college students to receive on-demand tutoring from other students with safety, efficiency, and most importantly, reliability in mind.






HIGH FIDELITY PROTOTYPE


01. Familiar Uber ecosystem with embedded promotions of tutors


• No sign up or download needed; Uber Tutors is already on the Uber app!
• Saves all your previous transactions all in one place


02. Easily narrow your search with strong visual elements


• See live tutor availability based on in-person or virtual preference
• Automatically sets each course as new color
• No time to learn a new app? - No worries! Intuitive interface for first-time users


03. Quickly find a tutor for whatever you need whenever you need it


• Easily search for your next tutor by course or location right away
• Have questions? Learn about your next tutor on their personalized page
• Centralized structure, so book away!


04. Sort and filter through to meet your needs


• Filter to your preferences
• Adjust location to meet in school buildings, ensuring safety for both parties
• Form connections. Build community.

INTEGRATING WITH THE UBER ECOSYSTEM


Looking into the future of adding this in-app feature, I consider how this would fit into the overall ecosystem. Uber has a separate app for Uber Eats to accomodate for individuals who only use one aspect of their services.



Uber's point system allows users to be rewarded for each dollar they spend which will benefit college students greatly since they often use the app for rides, food deliveries, and now, tutoring opportunities.

METRICS


If ya can't measure it, ya can't improve it.


Thinking back to our original problem, our goal was to help users 1. receive helpful tutoring services and 2. find these services through a fast and reliable way. These metrics would directly address both of these dimensions.


Checking both the tutors and tutees usability scale




To track whether users are able to find the right help, we could determine how helpful the services actually are by looking at how intuitive the platform is.

Satisfaction rate compared to other avenues




By testing with a subset of first-time users, we can gain valuable insights that help us determine if the tutoring service is not only fast and reliable, but exceeds their expectations.





REFLECTION


Looking back on my process and solution, there are a couple of areas I would have liked to improve.


Seek user feedback that wasn't friends or family

Given the timeline of a week for this project (I'm procrastinating on my accounting exam… still), my user research was limited. With more time, I would talk to randomized groups of my target audience to eliminate bias and gather more honest feedback.

Think more about privacy and safety

Privacy and safety are huge concerns for Uber, and finding more solutions to prevent these issues could further not only the Uber Tutors aspect but could be valuable for the Uber ecosystem as a whole.

Plan more ways to incorporate in the school system

U-M has a single system that holds course information which would be so beneficial to link to Uber Tutors. Users could have quick access to seeing when their big exams or projects are all within one click.
